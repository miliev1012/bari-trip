## Day Trips

### Zoosafari Fasanolandia — Day 2 (Sunday, March 29)
- **Distance:** ~1 hour from Bari via SS16
- **Rating:** ⭐ 4.5 (29.8k reviews)
- **Hours:** Mon–Fri 10 AM–1 PM / **Sat–Sun 10 AM–2 PM** — ARRIVE AT OPENING
- **Cost:** Family ticket ~€140 (buy online at [zoosafari.it](https://zoosafari.it))
- **Highlights:** Drive-through safari (giraffes, camels, zebras come to car), lemur walk-through (+€3), rides, shows
- **Tip:** Bring carrots for animal feeding. Perfect for a 3-year-old.

### Polignano a Mare — Day 4 (Tuesday, March 31)
- **Distance:** ~30 minutes via SS16 coastal road
- **Highlight:** Lama Monachile ⭐ 4.7 (20.5k) — Puglia's most iconic cliff cove
- **Cost:** Free (optional boat tour ~€15–20/adult)
- **Tips:** Bring beach shoes (rocky!), Old Town is stroller-friendly, upper town best for views

### Alberobello — Day 5 (Wednesday, April 1, optional)
- **Distance:** ~1 hour from Bari
- **Highlight:** Trulli Rione Monti ⭐ 4.8 (1.9k) — UNESCO village, fairy-tale cone-roofed houses
- **Cost:** Free to walk around
- **Best time:** 8–10 AM on a weekday — much fewer crowds
- **Note:** Allow extra time to return car if coming from here

## Grotte di Castellana (Day 4 optional add-on)

- **Distance:** ~40 min from Bari · ~20 min from Polignano a Mare
- **Short tour:** ~50 minutes · guided
- **Cost:** ~€15/adult · **children under 4 FREE**
- **Highlight:** 70m underground limestone caves — one of Italy's most spectacular cave systems
- **Note:** No strollers inside — bring baby carrier. Caves are 15°C year-round, pack a light layer.
- **Combo idea:** Morning in Polignano → Lunch in Polignano → Afternoon Castellana caves → Back to Bari

## Weather & Practical Tips

- **Sea:** Too cold for swimming but beautiful to look at
- **Stroller:** Bari Old Town has cobblestones but main alleys are manageable; Lungomare is perfectly flat
- **Parking in Bari:** Leave car at/near hotel for Old Town days and walk; parking is tricky & expensive in centro storico
- **Offline maps:** Download for Bari, Fasano, Polignano a Mare, and Alberobello before leaving
