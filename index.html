🍕 Must-Try Foods

🫓
Focaccia Barese
Soft, thick flatbread drenched in olive oil, topped with ripe tomatoes and olives. Puglia's everyday bread.
📍 Best at: Mastro Ciccio · Melo Coffee
🍝
Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa
The signature pasta of Puglia — ear-shaped pasta tossed with bitter turnip tops, anchovy, and chilli.
📍 Best at: La Cantina dello Zio · Antò
🥟
Panzerotto
A fried (or baked) pocket of dough stuffed with gooey mozzarella and tomato. Bari's street food king.
📍 Best at: Mastro Ciccio
🌀
Taralli
Crunchy ring-shaped crackers flavoured with fennel seeds and white wine. Perfect snack to bring home too.
📍 Found in any bakery or food shop
🧀
Burrata di Andria
Puglia's world-famous fresh cheese — a mozzarella shell filled with creamy stracciatella. Nothing like it elsewhere.
📍 Found at most restaurants
🍝
Spaghetti all'Assassina
Bari's fiery invention — spaghetti pan-fried at high heat with tomato and chilli until slightly charred and crispy.
📍 Best at: Le Nicchie

🍼

Travelling with a 3-year-old in Bari

Bari's Old Town (Bari Vecchia) has some cobblestones but is generally stroller-manageable on the main alleys. The Lungomare seafront is completely flat and perfect. Italian restaurants are very child-friendly — don't hesitate to ask for plain pasta or half-portions. Nap time in the car works perfectly with the day trip schedule.

🚗

Parking in Bari

Parking in the centro storico can be tricky and expensive. Leave the car at or near the hotel for Old Town days and walk. For day trips (Fasano, Polignano, Alberobello) the car is ideal. Most attractions have free or cheap parking lots.

🍽️

Italian Dining Times

Lunch is typically 12:30–2:30PM and dinner starts around 7:30–8PM. Restaurants often close between meals. For a toddler, arriving at 12:00 or 7:30 (when they open) means shorter waits and less noise. Always book dinner at La Cantina dello Zio ahead of time!
