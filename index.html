Monopoli
~36 min drive south · Coastal Adriatic town
🚗 36 min
Old Harbour · Icon
Porto Vecchio
The beating heart of Monopoli — blue fishing boats, fishermen mending nets, and a working lighthouse. Most atmospheric at sunset when the sky glows over the water. Toddlers love watching the boats up close.
🕐 Always open · Free
Castle · 16th Century
Castello di Carlo V
Pentagonal seaside castle with a panoramic tower overlooking the Adriatic. Hosts art exhibitions and photography festivals. The battlements and sea views are impressive — kids feel like they're in a pirate fortress.
🕐 Check local listings for current exhibitions
Cathedral · Baroque
Cattedrale della Madia
Monopoli's stunning baroque cathedral dedicated to the Madonna della Madia — according to legend, a miraculous icon arrived on a raft of timber from the sea. Beautiful interior, free to enter.
🕐 Daily · Free
Seafood · Harbour Views
La Locanda sul Porto
Charming restaurant right on the old harbour with stunning Adriatic views. Excellent fresh seafood and Puglian classics. Outdoor terrace over the port — arrive early or book ahead, very popular with locals and tourists alike.
🕐 Lunch & dinner · Reservations recommended
Seafood · Institution
Trattoria da Pierino l'Inglese
Family-run for over 50 years — a Monopoli institution. Fantastic fresh fish and seafood set menus at great value. No frills, just exceptional quality. One of the most beloved restaurants in town.
🕐 Lunch & dinner daily
Seafood · Old Port
Osteria Perricci
Old-school unfussy seafood right by the ancient port. Brilliant antipasti selection, salt-cod fritters, and fresh anchovies. No fuss, just incredibly fresh fish the way locals eat it.
🕐 Lunch & dinner · Closed Mon
Wine Bar · Aperitivo
Tuttoapposto Winebar
Street-side seating on the main square with an extensive Puglia wine list and antipasti platters. Perfect for a sunset aperitivo with people-watching on Piazza Garibaldi — a quintessentially Italian moment.
🕐 Afternoon & evening daily
Beach · Family Ideal
Cala Porta Vecchia
Sandy beach with a shallow sandy bottom — just 2 minutes from the historic centre. Calm, clear water ideal for small children. One of the best family beaches in Puglia. Beach clubs nearby with sunbeds if needed.
👶 Perfect for toddlers 🌊 Beach 🎟️ Free access

Short boat trips depart from the old port exploring sea caves, hidden coves, and the coastline south of Monopoli. Kids love it — calm Adriatic waters make it comfortable even for toddlers. Ask at the harbour for departures.

Alberobello
~1 hr drive · Optional in Day 5 itinerary
🚗 1 hr
UNESCO Village · In Day 5 plan
Trulli Rione Monti
⭐ 4.8 (1,856 reviews)
Fairy-tale cone-roofed stone houses. Your toddler will be amazed. Go early morning before the crowds — magical and free to walk around.
🕐 Always open (best 8–10AM) · Free
Trullo Museum · Interior
Trullo Sovrano
The only two-storey trullo in the world, open as a museum. Kids can explore tiny interior rooms, stone staircases, and conical ceilings. Quick and fun — ~20–30 min.
🕐 Daily 10AM–6PM · Small entry fee
👶 Great to explore 🏠 Indoor
Shopping · Souvenirs
Trulli Artisan Shops
Many trulli converted into artisan shops selling ceramics, miniature trulli figures, local foods and crafts. Your toddler will want the mini trullo toy!
🎟️ Free to browse
Neighbourhood · Authentic
Rione Aia Piccola
The quieter, less touristy trulli district — locals still live here among ~400 trulli. Much more atmospheric than Rione Monti in the morning before day-trippers arrive. Feels like stepping into real Puglian life.
🕐 Always open · Free
Church · Unique Architecture
Chiesa di Sant'Antonio
The only church in the world built entirely in the shape of a trullo. Built in 1927 when the area was already a National Monument — architects were forbidden to build in any other style. A genuinely remarkable sight.
🕐 Daily · Free to visit
Viewpoint · Panoramic
Belvedere Santa Lucia
The best panoramic viewpoint over the entire trulli skyline. A sea of conical stone rooftops stretching into the distance — the classic Alberobello postcard shot. Get there before 9AM to have it to yourself.
🕐 Always open · Free · Best early morning
Street Food · Focacceria
La Lira Focacceria
The best stop for focaccia and stuffed puccia sandwiches in Alberobello. Everything made fresh on the spot with local ingredients. The animated owner makes it a real experience — great for a quick family lunch.
🕐 Daily from late morning
Traditional · Puglian
La Cantina
Intimate stone-cellar restaurant — a local favourite for homemade orecchiette, tender lamb chops, and warm family-style service. The kind of place you linger over wine long after the plates are cleared.
🕐 Lunch & dinner · Book ahead recommended
Wine Bar · Local Produce
Trulli e Puglia Wine Bar
Outdoor seating on wine barrels along Via Monte Pertica. Local wines, cocktails, charcuterie boards, cheeses, and pasta dishes. A relaxed spot for an aperitivo or light lunch with great trulli views.
🕐 Daily from midday
Fine Dining · Michelin Star
Evo Ristorante
Alberobello's only Michelin-starred restaurant — contemporary Puglian cuisine with beautiful seasonal tasting menus. A special-occasion dinner. Pricier than the rest but worth it for a memorable evening.
🕐 Dinner only · Advance booking essential
⚠️ Book well ahead — fills up fast
Cooking Experience
Orecchiette Pasta-Making Class
Learn to make traditional orecchiette in a local's home — rolling semolina dough into Puglia's iconic "little ears" by hand. Authentic, fun, and usually followed by eating what you made. A memorable family experience.
👶 Kids love it 🏠 Indoor

Locorotondo
~1 hr drive · Valle d'Itria · 15 min from Alberobello
🚗 1 hr
Historic Centre · UNESCO Village
Centro Storico di Locorotondo
One of Italy's most beautiful villages (Borghi più belli d'Italia). Circular layout of whitewashed alleys, flower-filled balconies, and colourful doorways — completely pedestrian-only, making it perfectly safe for toddlers to wander. Best at golden hour.
🕐 Always open · Free
Viewpoint · Valle d'Itria
Belvedere di Locorotondo
Sweeping panoramic view over the Valle d'Itria — olive groves, vineyards, and trulli scattered across the hills as far as you can see. One of the most beautiful rural landscapes in all of southern Italy. Breathtaking at sunset.
🕐 Always open · Free
Church · Landmark
Chiesa Madre di San Giorgio
Locorotondo's most impressive monument — a creamy white neoclassical church with a distinctive dome and bell tower visible for miles. The interior is elegant and peaceful. Free to enter.
🕐 Daily · Free
Sunset Promenade
Via Nardelli — Sunset Walk
A circular street running along the southern edge of the historic centre — where locals gather every evening to watch the Valle d'Itria sky turn pink and orange. The best sunset viewpoint in the area. No crowds, just atmosphere.
🕐 Always open · Free
Traditional · Puglian
Osteria Il Rosone
Authentic Puglian trattoria using seasonal vegetables from their own garden. Excellent handmade strascinati pasta, local meats, and regional wines. Cosy and family-friendly — one of the best kitchens in the Valle d'Itria.
🕐 Lunch & dinner · Closed Mon
Traditional · Intimate
Ai Tre Santi
Lovely small restaurant with real local character. Regional Puglian specialities and a great selection of local wines. Best for a relaxed dinner when you want to linger. Charming setting in a narrow alley of the old town.
🕐 Dinner · Reservations recommended
Café · Piazza
Caffè della Villa
Right on Piazza Vittorio Emanuele near the main viewpoint. Cappuccino, pastries, cornetti, and pasticciotto (Puglian custard-filled pastry). Perfect morning stop before exploring the whitewashed streets. Good gelato too.
🕐 Daily from early morning
Artisan Dairy · Cheese Making
Caseificio Salatino
Watch mozzarella and fresh cheeses being made by hand — a surprisingly fascinating experience for children. Buy directly from the dairy: burrata, cacioricotta, scamorza. One of the most authentic local experiences near Locorotondo.
👶 Kids love watching 🏠 Indoor
Day Trip · Locorotondo
⚪ Whitewashed Alleys — Centro Storico
The narrow flower-lined alleys with whitewashed walls are endlessly photogenic. Early morning or late afternoon light is magical. A toddler running down an empty white alley makes for a perfect family shot.

Matera — Sassi UNESCO City
~1.5 hr drive · Basilicata · European Capital of Culture 2019
🚗 1.5 hr
UNESCO World Heritage · Cave City
Sassi di Matera — Sasso Barisano & Caveoso
One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world — over 7,000 years of history carved into the rock. The two Sassi neighbourhoods are a labyrinth of cave dwellings, rock churches, and ancient streets. Utterly unlike anywhere else on earth.
🕐 Always open · Free to walk through
⚠️ Not stroller-friendly — bring a baby carrier for the Sassi
Viewpoint · Iconic
Piazzetta Pascoli — Sassi Panorama
The most dramatic viewpoint over the entire Sassi — a balcony suspended above the ancient cave city with sweeping views across both neighbourhoods and the Gravina gorge beyond. The definitive Matera photograph. Go at sunrise or just before sunset.
🕐 Always open · Free
Cave Museum · Historic
Casa Grotta di Vico Solitario
A restored cave-dwelling with authentic period tools and furniture showing exactly how Matera families lived in the Sassi until the 1950s. Small, quick to visit, and genuinely fascinating — toddlers love exploring the tiny carved rooms. Small entry fee.
🕐 Daily 9AM–7PM · Small entry fee
👶 Kids love the cave rooms
Underground · Historic
Palombaro Lungo — Ancient Cistern
A vast 16th-century underground water cistern beneath Piazza Vittorio Veneto — one of the largest in Europe. Graceful walkways are suspended over the water in a cathedral-like cave space. Genuinely awe-inspiring. Accessed via a hidden entrance on the piazza.
🕐 Daily · Small entry fee
Sassi Views · Traditional
Keiv Ristorante
Terrace restaurant with one of the most spectacular views in Matera — the Sassi on the left, mountain peaks ahead, and the facade of San Pietro Caveoso church to the right. Excellent homemade orecchiette, burrata ravioli, and fresh antipasti. Reservations essential for a terrace table.
🕐 Lunch & dinner · Book ahead for terrace
Sassi Exploration · Family
Guided Sassi Walk with Kids
The Sassi is a giant maze that children naturally love exploring — narrow rock-cut passages, staircases carved into stone, ancient churches to peek into. Treasure hunt tours are available making it interactive for toddlers. Allow 2–3 hours at a relaxed pace.
👶 Use a baby carrier, not stroller 🌿 Outdoor
Museum · Cave Art
MUSMA — Museum of Sculpture
Contemporary sculpture museum set entirely inside a 17th-century cave palace. The combination of modern art and ancient cave architecture is striking. One of the most unusual museums in Italy — and the cave rooms keep kids fascinated.
🕐 Tue–Sun · €5

🍼

Travelling with a 3-year-old in Bari

Bari's Old Town (Bari Vecchia) has some cobblestones but is generally stroller-manageable on the main alleys. The Lungomare seafront is completely flat and perfect. Italian restaurants are very child-friendly — don't hesitate to ask for plain pasta or half-portions. Nap time in the car works perfectly with the day trip schedule.

🚗

Parking in Bari

Parking in the centro storico can be tricky and expensive. Leave the car at or near the hotel for Old Town days and walk. For day trips (Fasano, Polignano, Alberobello) the car is ideal. Most attractions have free or cheap parking lots.

🍽️

Italian Dining Times

Lunch is typically 12:30–2:30PM and dinner starts around 7:30–8PM. Restaurants often close between meals. For a toddler, arriving at 12:00 or 7:30 (when they open) means shorter waits and less noise. Always book dinner at La Cantina dello Zio ahead of time!

Everything you need to stay safe, smart, and stress-free
🚫

ZTL Warning — Avoid Fines with Your Rental Car

Bari's Old Town (Bari Vecchia) has a ZTL (Zona Traffico Limitato) — a restricted traffic zone monitored by cameras 24/7. Driving in by mistake with a rental car will result in an automatic fine sent weeks later.

  • Leave the car at or near the hotel when exploring the Old Town
  • Park at Parcheggio Via Trevisani or the seafront car parks nearby
  • Look for white/blue parking lines — white = free, blue = paid
  • Use Google Maps and search "parcheggio Bari" for real-time spots
  • Day trips (Fasano, Polignano, Alberobello) — no ZTL issues, park freely

🌧️ Rainy Day Backup Plan
Late March can bring showers — here's how to adapt each day
☔ Day 2 — Zoosafari in rain?
No problem! The drive-through safari is done entirely from your car. Pack a waterproof layer for the walking areas.
✅ Barely affected by rain
☔ Day 3 — Bari Old Town in rain?
The covered alleyways of Bari Vecchia are actually charming in light rain. Duck into the Basilica di San Nicola and the Cathedral — both completely indoor.
✅ Mostly fine — lots of indoor options
☔ Day 4 — Polignano in rain?
The cliff views at Lama Monachile are dramatic in moody weather. If heavy rain, swap with an extra Old Town day or visit the Museo Nazionale Archeologico di Taranto instead.
⚠️ Reschedule if heavy rain

Bari's beautiful covered 19th-century arcade in the new town. Perfect for a dry stroll with a stroller, cafés inside, and often hosts free exhibitions.
📍 Near the Teatro Petruzzelli

The Museo Nazionale Archeologico di Bari has fascinating Greek and Roman artefacts from Puglia. Toddler-friendly — big open halls and lots to look at.
📍 Bari city centre
